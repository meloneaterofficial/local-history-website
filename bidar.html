Welcome to Bidar

Bidar (/biːd̪ər/) is a city in the north-eastern part of Karnataka state in India. It is the headquarters of Bidar district, which borders Maharashtra and Telangana.

It is a rapidly urbanising city in the wider Bidar Metropolitan area. The city is well known for its many sites of architectural, historical and religious importance. Bidar City have a population of more than 2 lakh and are likely to get upgraded to municipal corporation in the next 5 years.

Being located at the farthest of around 744 km (462 mi) from the state capital Bangalore, it has been neglected by the state government for a long time. However, owing to its rich heritage, the city has a prominent place on the Archaeological Map of India. Picturesquely perched on the Deccan plateau, the Bidar fort is more than 500 years old and still standing strong.

LOCAL LORE

In the heart of Bidar, nestled within the ancient walls of the city, a banyan tree stands tall and majestic. This tree, known as the “Whispering Banyan,” is said to hold secrets and stories that have echoed through the ages. Legend has it that the banyan tree was a silent observer of the city’s rise and fall. During times of peace, its leaves rustled with stories of love, laughter, and prosperity. But during times of turmoil, it seemed to share a deeper understanding of the human heart’s struggles. Locals believe that if you visit the tree during the golden hours of dawn, when the first rays of sunlight kiss its branches, you might hear faint whispers carried by the wind. These whispers are said to hold the voices of past generations, sharing their wisdom, hopes, and dreams. Generations of Bidar’s residents have gathered beneath the Whispering Banyan to seek solace, inspiration, and a connection to their roots. It’s said that the tree’s embrace is a reminder that Bidar’s history is interwoven with the stories of its people. As the sun sets over the city, the banyan tree’s silhouette against the sky stands as a silent testament to the enduring spirit of Bidar and the tales it carries within its leafy embrace.
